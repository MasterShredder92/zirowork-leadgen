# ZiroWork Page-Specific Hormozi Operator Map

## Purpose

This map must be embedded into the ZiroWork Hormozi/ALAN operator skill so agents do not lose context while working in specific app pages, folders, routes, or feature areas. The agent must not treat ZiroWork pages as neutral UI screens. Each page exists to create, protect, prove, or recover revenue for a music school client.

## Global Page Entry Lock

Before entering any page, route, folder, or feature area, the agent must stop and answer the page’s operating objective. The agent is not allowed to code, design, write copy, add data fields, create UI, or recommend changes until the page objective is clear.

The page entry lock is:

```md
I am entering [Page]. This page exists to answer: [operator question].
The revenue leak is: [specific leak].
The proof this page must create is: [specific proof].
The next action it must force is: [specific action].
If I cannot answer this, I do not understand the page yet.
```

## Global Mindset Lock

The agent must operate from these source principles while working on every page:

| Principle | ZiroWork meaning |
|---|---|
| "A focused fool can accomplish more than a distracted genius." | Do not scatter the page across nice-to-have features. Make it force the single highest-leverage action. |
| "People do not have shorter attention spans; they have higher standards." | Weak UI, weak copy, weak proof, and vague status states fail the page. The page must be clear enough for an operator under pressure. |
| "A lead is a person you can contact." | Leads are not rows. They are reachable humans with a next move, consent/source context, and revenue potential. |
| "The market does not pay for effort. It pays for outcomes." | The page must prove bookings, enrollments, saved labor, avoided losses, or operational control. |
| "The more ways you measure, the more ways you can win." | Every page must produce measurable state, not vague activity. |
| "If you want to conquer churn, master onboarding." | Client success starts when the first installed workflow creates proof, not when an account is created. |

## Page Operating Doctrine

| Page | Operator question | Mindset lock | Agent command | Required output state | Hard stop |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Command Center | What needs attention right now across all clients? | Think: **focused operator war room**. A distracted dashboard loses money. | Rank only the issues that can cost revenue today: stuck leads, broken integrations, overdue escalations, missed trials, failed workflows, and at-risk clients. | A ranked action list with owner, urgency, revenue risk, client impact, and next move. | Do not build a pretty dashboard. If it does not force action, cut it. |
| Clients | Which schools are live, onboarding, healthy, stuck, or at risk? | Think: **portfolio value protection**. Every client either proves value or creates churn risk. | Treat each client as an asset with stage, risk, proof, bottleneck, and retention move. | Each client has stage, health, bottleneck, last proof event, next action, and risk reason. | Do not show vanity client lists without operational state. |
| Onboarding | What is missing before a client can launch? | Think: **churn is beaten before the client feels confused**. Account setup is not success. | Drive the client to first workflow proof: access, assets, offer, workflow training, approval rules, replay, and logs. | A launch checklist tied to first tenant workflow proof. | Do not mark onboarding complete until the first workflow can run and log proof. |
| Campaigns | Which piano/guitar/voice/drum funnels are running and producing? | Think: **demand math, not marketing theater**. Leads only matter if they become trials and enrollments. | Judge campaigns by source → lead → booked → showed → enrolled → revenue. | Campaign status shows offer, audience, channel, spend, leads, booked, showed, enrolled, cost per outcome, and next test. | Do not optimize ads without downstream booking and enrollment proof. |
| Pages | Which landing pages and signup pages are draft, ready, live, or broken? | Think: **Grand Slam Offer filter**. A page must make the parent feel stupid saying no to the next step. | Force offer clarity, proof, low friction, CTA, tracking, and lead route. | Each page has status, offer, CTA, proof, load/track status, form route, and issue state. | Do not ship pages with weak offer, unclear CTA, broken tracking, or disconnected lead routing. |
| Leads | Who is in the pipeline and what is the next move? | Think: **ALAN lead-working machine**. A lead without a next action is money leaking. | Every lead must have speed, status, next touch, owner/AI state, and outcome path. | Each lead has source, stage, last touch, next action, owner, consent/source note, and risk. | Do not let leads sit without a next move. Dead pipeline equals lost revenue. |
| Conversations | What is being said, what has AI handled, and what needs review? | Think: **closer with guardrails**. Automation can assist; it cannot freelance trust. | Let AI handle approved routine follow-up. Escalate ambiguity, emotion, pricing, schedule conflicts, refunds, complaints, and edge cases. | Conversation state shows AI actions, customer intent, sentiment/risk, next message, and escalation flag. | Do not let automation resolve sensitive conversations without approval. |
| Escalations | What should not be handled by automation? | Think: **trust protection**. Escalations are not failures; they are where brand damage is prevented. | Prioritize cases that can hurt trust, money, safety, retention, or compliance. | Every escalation has reason, severity, owner, recommended action, due time, and resolution log. | Do not bury escalations. Do not let AI resolve high-risk cases without approval. |
| Bookings | What trials are requested, scheduled, paid, no-showed, or rescheduled? | Think: **show-rate economics**. Booking turns interest into intent. Delay kills enrollment. | Reduce response time, confirm slots, remind, recover no-shows, and update status. | Booking state shows requested slot, confirmed slot, teacher/location, payment status, reminders, show/no-show, and recovery step. | Do not build a generic calendar. Build trial conversion control. |
| Enrollments | What became revenue and what was lost? | Think: **scoreboard**. If it did not become revenue or a known loss reason, the system is blind. | Prove what converted, what failed, why it failed, and what follow-up remains. | Enrollment state shows trial result, sold/lost reason, revenue value, attribution, follow-up, and retention implication. | Do not claim performance value without enrollment proof. |
| Reporting | Why should each client keep paying ZiroWork? | Think: **retention proof and pricing power**. Reports must show money recovered, labor saved, and risk avoided. | Convert activity into ROI, proof, bottlenecks, and next growth move. | Reports show leads worked, trials booked, shows, enrollments, lost reasons, automation wins, human saves, and ROI math. | Do not report activity. Report outcomes and retained value. |
| Automation Rules | What should AI say, do, pause, or escalate? | Think: **controlled scale**. The money is made by leverage; the business is protected by boundaries. | Define exact triggers, allowed actions, blocked actions, approvals, failure states, and audit logs. | Rules include trigger, allowed action, blocked action, approval need, failure state, and audit log. | Do not create broad automation without pause/escalation policy. |
| Assets | What logos, photos, teacher bios, testimonials, offers, and brand voice are available? | Think: **offer ammunition**. Assets exist to increase trust, proof, conversion, and speed. | Tag assets by what they help sell or prove. | Asset library tags assets by client, instrument, location, usage rights, proof type, and readiness. | Do not store random files. Every asset needs conversion use or operational purpose. |
| Integrations | Which forms, phone numbers, QUO/SMS, payment links, calendars, and email routes are connected or broken? | Think: **revenue plumbing**. If integrations break, money leaks silently. | Every connection needs status, last test, failure reason, owner, and repair move. | Integration state shows connected/broken, last sync, test result, failure reason, owner, and repair action. | Do not mark connected without a test event and visible result. |
| Settings | Global ZiroWork/operator settings. | Think: **guardrail control**. Settings should protect tenants, approvals, roles, privacy, and defaults. | Expose only settings that change system behavior, access, safety, or tenant operation. | Settings show roles, tenant boundaries, approval defaults, privacy rules, brand rules, and operator defaults. | Do not turn settings into a junk drawer. |
| Insights | Bottlenecks, wins, client risk, and suggested next actions. | Think: **operator intelligence**. Insight without action is noise. | Rank bottlenecks, wins, churn risks, revenue opportunities, and next best actions with evidence. | Insights rank what matters, why it matters, evidence, revenue impact, and next move. | Do not generate vague AI commentary. Every insight must point to a decision or action. |

## Folder/File Behavior Rule

If the repo folder or file maps to one of these pages, the agent must apply that page’s doctrine before code, copy, data model, UI, or workflow changes. The agent must state the page’s operator question, define the revenue/proof function, and preserve the dashboard → extension event/state → backend action → Supabase tenant-owned row/table → user-visible result connection map.

## ZiroWork Page Build Ranking

The agent must prioritize pages that prove the MVP loop first. The initial order is Onboarding, Leads, Bookings, Enrollments, Reporting, Integrations, Automation Rules, Command Center, then the remaining operational pages. Campaigns and Pages matter, but they must not distract from the manual train → compile → replay → log → repair loop unless the task is explicitly about acquisition.

When working on a page, the agent must output:

```md

[Short quote/principle and what it means for this page.]

[The minimum data/state/actions the page must expose.]

## Hard Stop
[What must not be built, hidden, or skipped.]

## Next Build Move
[One concrete file/task/action.]
```
