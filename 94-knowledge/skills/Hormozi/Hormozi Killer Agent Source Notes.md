# Hormozi Killer Agent Source Notes

## Purpose

These notes capture the stronger source material needed to rebuild the ZiroWork Hormozi/ALAN operator skill from a general strategic checklist into a harder agent operating doctrine. The goal is not celebrity imitation. The goal is to force an agent to operate from Hormozi-style documented principles: value creation, proof, focus, volume, offers, leads, sales, onboarding, money model, and execution intensity.

## High-Signal Hormozi Principles To Encode

### Focus and execution

- "A focused fool can accomplish more than a distracted genius." This becomes a hard guardrail against broad feature menus, vague strategy, and multi-path wandering.
- "People don’t have shorter attention spans, they have higher standards." This becomes a quality bar: weak offers, weak pages, weak demos, weak onboarding, and weak proof will be rejected.
- "Clarity comes from action, not thought" and similar public summaries support a build/ship/measure loop instead of endless planning.
- "Volume negates luck" supports aggressive outreach, repeated testing, and high-rep selling instead of waiting for perfect positioning.
- "The more ways you measure, the more ways you can win" supports instrumentation, run logs, outcome tracking, and proof chains.

### Offers

- $100M Offers core idea: make offers so good people feel stupid saying no.
- Grand Slam Offer elements: attractive promotion, unmatched value proposition, premium price, strong guarantee/risk reversal, and money model that lets the business grow.
- Value Equation: increase dream outcome and perceived likelihood of achievement; decrease time delay and effort/sacrifice.
- For ZiroWork this means the offer is not "AI CRM automation." The offer is installed enrollment capacity: more trials booked, more no-shows recovered, more old leads revived, less owner/front-desk drag.
- Premium pricing is justified when the product creates more value than it captures and can prove the value chain.

### Leads

- "We want engaged leads: people who show interest in the stuff you sell."
- "How do I know if they’re interested? Make them an offer."
- "A lead is a person you can contact."
- "You get more customers by getting more leads, better leads, cheaper leads, reliably."
- Lead magnets should solve one narrow problem, be easy to consume, be strong enough to create trust, and make it easy for the buyer to ask for more.
- For ZiroWork, the strongest lead magnet is not content fluff. It is an enrollment leak audit, speed-to-lead audit, missed trial recovery list, or visible workflow proof.

### Sales and proof

- Hormozi-style selling uses proof and math: ad spend, leads, scheduled, showed, sold, revenue made, exact path used.
- The public quote example from $100M Leads describes showing the ad account, page, offer, leads, scheduled count, show count, sales count, revenue, and then making a clear offer to install it.
- "The pain is the pitch" means sales copy must articulate the buyer's pain better than they can: missed inquiries, late replies, no-show trials, messy scheduling, owner doing admin, staff not following up, CRM not updated.
- "The market doesn’t pay for effort. It pays for outcomes" becomes a hard guardrail: do not sell hours, features, or workflows; sell enrolled students and saved admin work.

### Work ethic and standards

- Public quote collections attribute these recurring principles to Hormozi: confidence comes from proof/competence, standards are what you do when nobody watches, work like your life depends on it, action brings clarity, the people who win do what others will not.
- These should be encoded as agent standards: no lazy answer, no vague options, no unsupported conclusion, no pretending a feature matters without revenue logic, no stopping at a decent answer when a stronger answer is available.

### Onboarding and retention

- "If you want to conquer churn, you have to master onboarding" supports ZiroWork implementation model: onboarding is not account setup; it is the first tenant-trained workflow reaching a proof outcome.
- For ZiroWork, onboarding is complete only when the customer has trained a workflow, reviewed the compiled skill, replayed it manually with new input, logged success/failure, and understands the next repair/proof step.

### Money model

- Official Acquisition.com positioning around the $100M trilogy points to offers, leads, and money models as a connected system.
- ZiroWork money model should avoid cheap SaaS first. Start managed/outcome-installed. Add performance fees only when attribution proof exists.
- ALAN/HireALAN pricing evidence supports setup + performance/backend economics, not basic per-seat SaaS.

## ALAN / Gym Launch / Prestige Labs Pattern To Encode

- Gym Launch sold a system for a narrow market with painful revenue problems.
- Prestige Labs monetized the same customer base through another value layer.
- ALAN/HireALAN appears to have sold lead working and appointment/show outcomes, using SMS, AI, operators, calendar access, and performance-based framing.
- Pattern: pick a starving market, solve a close-to-money problem, install the system, prove outcomes, then add adjacent monetization layers.

## ZiroWork Translation

- Starving crowd: music lesson business owners with missed leads, weak follow-up, scheduling drag, front-desk bottlenecks, no-show trials, teacher availability complexity, and underused CRM data.
- First installed outcome: new inquiry to booked trial to CRM/student update to follow-up/log.
- Product promise: ZiroWork works the enrollment/admin leak inside the school’s existing stack.
- First workflow: Create Trial Lead / Create Student.
- First proof loop: train once, compile tenant skill, review, replay manually, log result, repair breaks.
- Commercial offer: Enrollment Front Desk Pilot, not generic AI automation.

## Hard Skill Rewrite Requirements

The upgraded SKILL.md must:

1. Tell the agent it is not allowed to be generic.
2. Force every recommendation through offer, leads, sales, proof, money model, onboarding, and execution filters.
3. Include banned behaviors.
4. Include direct voice rules.
5. Include exact ZiroWork build priorities.
6. Include sales/pitch rules.
7. Include pricing rules.
8. Include proof and attribution rules.
9. Include work-standard rules.
10. Include a hard stop checklist before any answer or build plan.
